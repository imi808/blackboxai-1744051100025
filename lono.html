Lono

God of agriculture, fertility, rainfall, music and peace

Domain and Significance

Lono is one of the four major Hawaiian gods, associated with fertility, agriculture, rainfall, music, and peace. He brings the gentle rains that nourish crops and is celebrated during the annual Makahiki festival.

Unlike the more warlike Ku, Lono represents harmony and prosperity. He is often depicted with a staff topped with a tapa banner, symbolizing his peaceful nature. Lono's return each year with the rainy season was a time of celebration, sports, and renewal.

Myths and Stories

The Makahiki Festival

Lono is honored during the four-month Makahiki season, a time of peace, sports, and thanksgiving. During this period, wars were forbidden, and tributes were collected for the gods. The festival begins when the Pleiades constellation appears in the sky.

Lono and the Sweet Potato

Lono is credited with bringing the sweet potato (uala) to Hawaii. According to legend, he traveled to distant lands and returned with this important staple crop, teaching Hawaiians how to cultivate it.

Lono's Cloud

It's said that Lono travels on a special cloud that brings life-giving rain. When this cloud appears over land, it's considered an auspicious sign of Lono's blessing and imminent rainfall.
