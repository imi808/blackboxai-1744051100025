Kane

The creator god of life, sunlight, and fresh water

Domain and Significance

Kane is one of the four major Hawaiian gods and is considered the highest of the four. He is the god of creation, sunlight, forests, and fresh water.

Kane is associated with life and procreation. In Hawaiian mythology, he created the first man and woman, and all living things. He is often depicted with a staff or spear, symbolizing his role as a creator and protector.

Symbols and Representations

Natural Elements

  • Sunlight
  • Fresh water (springs, streams)
  • Forests and trees
  • Rainbows

Sacred Objects

  • Staff or spear
  • Kapa (bark cloth)
  • Red feathers

Myths and Stories

Creation of the World

According to legend, Kane emerged from the primordial darkness (Po) and created the heavens, earth, and all living things. He worked with Ku and Lono to bring order to the universe.

The First Humans

Kane shaped the first man (Kumu-honua) from red earth and the first woman (Lalo-honua) from his rib. He breathed life into them and gave them the sacred forests as their home.

The Water of Life

Kane is said to have created the first fresh water springs by striking the ground with his staff. These springs were considered sacred and were places of healing and prayer.
