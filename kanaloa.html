Kanaloa

God of the ocean, the underworld, and healing

Domain and Significance

Kanaloa is one of the four major Hawaiian deities, associated with the ocean, long-distance voyaging, and the underworld. He is often paired with Kane as a complementary force.

While Kane represents life and creation, Kanaloa represents the mysteries of the deep ocean and the afterlife. He is also associated with healing and was invoked by Hawaiian kahuna (priests) for medicinal practices.

Symbols and Representations

Natural Elements

  • The ocean (especially deep waters)
  • Squid and octopus
  • Darkness and night
  • Moon phases

Sacred Objects

  • Kala (whale tooth pendant)
  • Voyaging canoes
  • Black kapa cloth
  • Healing stones

Myths and Stories

The Voyage to Kahiki

Kanaloa is credited with teaching Hawaiians the art of long-distance voyaging. He guided the first canoes to Hawaii from the mythical land of Kahiki, using the stars as his map.

The Underworld

Kanaloa rules over Milu, the Hawaiian underworld, where souls of the dead reside. Unlike Western concepts of hell, Milu is not a place of punishment but rather a shadowy reflection of the living world.

The Healing Waters

Kanaloa is associated with healing springs and medicinal knowledge. Many Hawaiian healing chants invoke his name to draw upon his power to cure illnesses and injuries.
